Market Opportunity

Stillwater, Oklahoma sits at an economic inflection point. Home to Oklahoma State University — the state's largest university system — the city combines a rapidly growing student population, a highly educated resident base, and a booming college-town economy that punches well above its weight. The conditions for a thriving Micro Center location are already in place.

Growing Population

Stillwater's population has grown more than 25% since 2000, rising from roughly 39,600 to an estimated 49,500 residents. The city currently grows at approximately 0.86% annually — outpacing comparable-sized U.S. cities. OSU Stillwater set an all-time enrollment record of 27,655 students in Fall 2025, its second consecutive record year, with undergraduate enrollment approaching 23,000. A record freshman class of over 5,000 arrived in Fall 2024, two years ahead of OSU's own strategic goal.

Strong College Town Economy

OSU is Stillwater's largest employer and the dominant driver of the local economy. A 2024 economic impact report found that OSU generated over $3.82 billion for the Oklahoma economy in fiscal year 2024, supporting nearly 27,000 jobs statewide and producing $110.3 million in state and local tax revenue. University workers, students, and visitors spend $129 million annually on restaurants, retail, and taxable goods — generating $4.5 million in sales tax for the City of Stillwater each year.

Average student spending in the local economy runs $4,300 per year for on-campus residents and $10,200 per year for off-campus students. With over 23,000 undergraduates and a growing graduate college of nearly 5,000, consumer spending in Stillwater is substantial and consistent year-round.

High Purchasing Power Segments

More than 48% of Stillwater residents hold at least a bachelor's degree — the highest educated workforce in Oklahoma. That educational concentration translates directly into higher technology affinity and discretionary spending on electronics, components, and computing hardware. Stillwater's cost of living sits approximately 10% below the national average, meaning purchasing power stretches further here than in comparable markets.
