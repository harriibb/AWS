Demographics Snapshot

Stillwater's demographics are uniquely well-suited for a Micro Center location. The city's population profile — young, educated, tech-forward, and concentrated — mirrors the customer base that Micro Center has successfully served in comparable university markets. Three distinct segments drive the opportunity: students, young professionals, and families.

Students

With 27,655 students enrolled at OSU Stillwater as of Fall 2025, the student population alone represents a captive customer base of unprecedented density for a city of Stillwater's size. The median age in Stillwater is just 23.9 years — one of the lowest in Oklahoma — reflecting the overwhelming student influence on city demographics. Undergraduate enrollment is approaching 23,000, with a record freshman class of over 5,000 arriving in Fall 2024. These students are actively purchasing laptops, monitors, cables, components, and peripherals for coursework, gaming, and personal projects throughout the academic year.

Young Professionals

Beyond the student body, Stillwater hosts a significant young professional population tied to OSU's research enterprise, Stillwater Medical Center, Meridian Technology Center, and the Oklahoma Technology & Research Park — a 170-acre campus for technology startups and research spinoffs. Employment in Stillwater grew 4.7% from 2023 to 2024, adding over 1,000 jobs. This professional class represents consistent, year-round purchasing power that extends beyond the academic calendar.

Families

Stillwater's family demographic benefits from a cost of living approximately 10% below the national average, making discretionary spending on technology more accessible than in comparable markets. More than 48% of Stillwater residents hold at least a bachelor's degree — the highest educated workforce in Oklahoma — and family households represent a reliable market for home computing, gaming systems, smart home devices, and repair services. With over 680,000 annual visitors to OSU events and a regional draw area of approximately 85,000 people, a Micro Center location would serve well beyond Stillwater's city limits.

Economic Benefits
What Micro Center Brings

A Micro Center location in Stillwater would generate measurable, lasting economic benefits for the city, Payne County, and the broader north-central Oklahoma region. These are not speculative — they mirror the documented economic impact of retail anchors in comparable university markets. Based on Oklahoma's sales tax rate of 4.5% and Stillwater's local rate, a store generating $20M–$30M in annual revenue — conservative for a Micro Center in a market this size — would deliver significant recurring public revenue.

Job Creation

A full-size Micro Center location typically employs 100–200 people, including retail associates, technical staff, service technicians, and management. In Stillwater's market, this would represent a meaningful expansion of the retail employment base. Oklahoma's Quality Jobs incentive program offers qualifying businesses quarterly cash payments of up to 5% of new payrolls for up to 10 years, making Stillwater an especially attractive location for new retail employment investment. OSU students would benefit directly from part-time employment opportunities within walking or biking distance of campus.

Local Tax Revenue

Stillwater generates $4.5 million annually in sales tax from OSU-related retail activity alone. A Micro Center store would add materially to this base. Oklahoma's combined state and local sales tax rate in Stillwater is approximately 9%, meaning every $10 million in annual sales generates roughly $900,000 in combined tax revenue. A store at the lower end of typical Micro Center volume ($20M/yr) would contribute approximately $1.8 million annually to state and local coffers — funding schools, roads, and public services. The City of Stillwater has active Tax Increment Financing infrastructure and business incentive programs ready to support qualifying retail development.

Increased Retail Traffic

One of the documented effects of anchor retail stores is the halo effect on surrounding businesses. OSU draws 680,000 visitors annually to sports and academic events, with those visitors spending $47 million in Stillwater. A Micro Center location — accessible from both Highway 51 (the Stillwater–Tulsa corridor) and Highway 177 (the OKC route) — would attract regional customers from Ponca City, Perry, Guthrie, and communities throughout north-central Oklahoma who currently have no nearby tech retail option. This regional draw amplifies the direct sales impact and benefits the broader Stillwater retail economy.
